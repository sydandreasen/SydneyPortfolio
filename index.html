Research at UNO's Biomechanics Research Building

After looking at short excerpts about each faculty's research, the team that spoke to me the most was that of Dr. Brian Knarr, which I began volunteering for. Dr. Knarr was interested in my potential after little time together and decided to offer me a job! As of January 2019, I have been a hard-working and dependable undergraduate student research assistant on Dr. Knarr's research team. I am always excited to get experience working on different projects; when students join the team, they tend to notice that I work on as many, or more, projects as each of the graduate students on the team.

Research on Dr. Knarr's Research Team

  • About a dozen students at a time.
  • Great atmosphere.
  • Productive teamwork.
  • Supportive mentorship and guidance.
  • Sharing expertises.
  • Rehabilitation Research for stroke and total knee arthroplasty.
  • Strength, gait kinetics and kinematics, and upper body motor control.

To learn more about the research done on Dr. Knarr's team and others in the Biomechanics Research Building, click here.

Research I Have Personally Worked On

MATLAB analysis of clinical trial data in collaboration with researchers at the University of Delaware.
This project entailed:

  • Reorganization of a large table of step data by individual and then by day.
  • Linear and non-linear analysis regarding step activity data.
  • Comparisons among step data and against weather data.
  • Sophisticated graphing.
  • Statistics and data interpretation.
  • Communication with collaborators.
  • Scientific writing.
