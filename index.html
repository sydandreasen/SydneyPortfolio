Summer 2020 at PayPal

The Overall Experience

From day one, the PayPal team was so welcoming and ready to help. It was such a great experience to work for a company who truly really practices what they preach. The PayPal team lists, and lives by, their core values: Wellness, Inclusion, Collaboration, and Innovation. In 12 short weeks, I saw many examples of each and every one of these values being carried out. Here are just a few examples:

  • Wellness
    • Meetings regularly include a check-up on each other making sure everyone is doing well, and we are reminded to get up from our home office and take breaks, and stay just as active during the day as if there were an office to walk around.
    • Intern meetups included yoga-inspired stretch sessions.
    • Periodic virtual meetups occurred for inspiration for being active, cooking recipes, and keeping kids active for those teammates who were experiencing the unprecendented times of homeschooling kids while trying to work full time from home.
  • Inclusion
    • Interns are respected for their ideas, work, and questions, and are included in meetings along their Full-Time Employee teammates.
    • Diversity & Inclusion Groups are being added and exisiting ones are always growing in their activity and scope.
    • Diversity & Inclusion Groups educate about and promote the inclusion of their main focused group, but they are also inclusive in their interaction with the other groups at PayPal to be the best allies they can to their fellow groups.
    • Beyond its own internal community, PayPal has reached out to support inclusion in society by, for example, donating large sums of money to support black-owned businesses.
  • Collaboration
    • Teammates are always ready, willing, and wanting to help. My PayPal colleagues would make time to troubleshoot with me and offer a suggestion of who would be a good resource for help if they were unable to resolve the issue, further expanding my network.
    • My entire project was entirely collaborative, working alongside and coordinating with two other interns to build an app from the group up.
    • Interns were given the opportunity to participate in a two-week long event to work with the Kiva merchants paired with PayPal. We discussed problem statements presented by small businesses that were brought about largely due to their transition onto fully online sales during the COVID-19 pandemic. We were able to learn about these small businesses, brainstorm with them about ideas to improve their business. After getting on the same page with our merchant, our small groups of interns developer proposals for the merchants to dive deeper into solutions to their problems. Check out the great small business I worked with, Ostri Studio, here.
  • Innovation
    • Interns, and all teammates, are encouraged to propose ideas for new innovative ideas the company may want to pursue regardless of whether or not that idea falls in that employee's organization or expertise.
    • For my project itself, we were given very basic instructions of what the end product should do or contain, and the managers left a great deal of the design and implementation up to us. This gave us a chance to more critically think about the impact our application would have on the company and develop a fuller understanding, simply because we were left free to design and not given a specific formula to follow.

My Work

I utilized Adobe XD to prototype a UI and then worked in React to build an application from the group up alongside two other interns. Our app took previous internal reporting methodologies and provided a streamlined app that showed data in a more visual and easily understandable format, while providing the opportunity for users to filter, sort, export, and interact with the data in many ways to maximize the personalized value of the data presented on the screen.

This project advanced my professional career greatly by learning how to build React apps, work with libraries, work with a database and make SELECT, UPDATE, INSERT, and DELETE queries, and utilizing an Express server during development to allow the front and back ends of the application to interact. Additionally, this project facilitated my learning of various areas that I wanted to learn for personal projects and made the list of things to learn feel much easier, especially with all the support I had around me. It blows my mind to think about how much I learned and absorbed over those 12 weeks, much of which has become second nature. My improvement was evident throughout the internship and especially from beginning to end as I write this section into my Portfolio site, and just the idea that I wrote this whole site with essentially two files, one .html and one .css, seems crazy now!

The Intern Program

Even being entirely virtual this summer, the University Programs Team did a great job of organizing some great events for us interns. And the virtual experience was an advantage in many ways, connecting us to people from all over the nation and world, rather than being focused on networking within just our local office. The team organized many business and executive speaker events where we had the opportunity to learn more about PayPal and receive advice from many successful individuals. Other meetings focused on soft skill building, like how to pitch a proposal and how to show promote yourself on LinkedIn. Interns across the nation met up each week for updates and bonding activities. One-on-one intern meetups were also facilitated each week to allow more personal interactions with our colleages near and far. Besides organizing many opportunities for us to network, learn, and build our skillsets, the University Programs Team offered incentives to attend and engage in events, which we could rack up toward earning prizes. Additionally, the team sent all interns a fantastic swag package for interns to show off their PayPal gear and experience.

Research at UNO's Biomechanics Research Building
